About the Project

This simulation brings to life Conway’s Game of Life—a zero-player game where cells on a grid live, die, or multiply based on mathematical rules. Our platform blends engaging front-end visuals with robust back-end capabilities to provide an immersive and educational experience.

Why Use This Simulation?

Our Game of Life simulation offers more than just entertainment:

  • Understand fundamental concepts in cellular automata and complexity.
  • Experiment with emergent behavior and see how simple rules create complex outcomes.
  • Save and share your creations with others.

About the Development Team

This Conway’s Game of Life Simulation was developed by Team 14 as part of a collaborative web programming course at Georgia State University. Our team combined creative design, coding expertise, and strong teamwork to deliver a full-stack simulation experience.

  • Sophie Nguyen: Team leader and lead developer, responsible for backend integration and simulation logic.
  • Grant Boddy: Front-end developer and tester, focused on grid UI, controls, and usability.
  • Rida Hameed Syeda: UI/UX designer and front-end contributor, ensuring a responsive and visually appealing interface.
  • All members: Participated in team meetings, debugging sessions, documentation, and final presentations.
